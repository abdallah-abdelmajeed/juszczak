About Me

I am a Full Professor at Poznań University of Life Sciences, specializing in environmental science with a focus on greenhouse gas emissions, carbon cycling, and wetland ecosystems. My research combines field measurements, laboratory analyses, and modeling to understand ecosystem processes and their response to environmental changes.

Research Interests

Peatland Climate Manipulation

Leading research on climate manipulation sites on peatlands, investigating how reduced precipitation and increased temperature impact peatland vegetation, greenhouse gas fluxes, and carbon balances.

Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Studying carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide fluxes from various ecosystems, with a particular focus on wetlands, peatlands, and agricultural lands.

Remote Sensing Applications

Utilizing hyperspectral remote sensing and sun-induced fluorescence (SIF) to monitor peatland vegetation traits, photosynthetic activity, and ecosystem responses to environmental changes.

Carbon Cycling

Investigating carbon sequestration, storage, and release processes in natural and managed ecosystems, with emphasis on peatland carbon dynamics under changing climate conditions.

Projects

ReVersal - Restoring peatlands of the nemoral zone

2022-2025

Restoring peatlands of the nemoral zone under conditions of varying water supply and quality. Project funded by National Science Centre of Poland (NCN) No. 2021/03/Y/ST10/00093 BiodivRestore. Project contractor, member of the consortium.

Climate Manipulation on Peatlands

2020-Present

Principal Investigator for three climate manipulation sites on peatlands, using Open Top Chambers (OTCs) and infrared radiators to study the effects of increased temperature and reduced precipitation on peatland ecosystems.

Remote Sensing of Sun-Induced Fluorescence (SIF)

2018-Present

Application of remote sensing techniques to monitor photosynthetic activity and vegetation stress in peatland ecosystems, with a focus on sun-induced fluorescence as an indicator of ecosystem health.

Greenhouse Gas Flux Monitoring in Wetlands

2015-Present

Long-term monitoring of methane, carbon dioxide, and nitrous oxide fluxes in temperate peatlands of Central Europe, using eddy covariance and chamber-based measurement techniques.

Peatland Vegetation Response to Climate Change

2017-2022

Investigation of how peatland vegetation structure, composition, morphology, and photosynthetic activity respond to changing climate conditions, with particular focus on Sphagnum species.

Teaching

Ecosystem Ecology and Biogeochemistry

Graduate course focusing on carbon and nutrient cycling in terrestrial ecosystems, with emphasis on wetlands and peatlands as critical components of the global carbon cycle.

Environmental Monitoring and Remote Sensing

Advanced course on the application of remote sensing techniques for monitoring ecosystem processes, vegetation traits, and environmental changes in natural and managed landscapes.

Climate Change and Ecosystem Responses

Graduate seminar examining how different ecosystems respond to climate change, with case studies from peatlands, wetlands, and agricultural systems.

Field Methods in Environmental Science

Hands-on course teaching students techniques for measuring greenhouse gas fluxes, vegetation properties, and environmental parameters in field settings.
